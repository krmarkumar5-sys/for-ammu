For you, Ammu

A few things I keep telling God about you

M

y Ammu, I don't say this because it's a nice thing to say — I say it because it's true every time I look at you: you are beautiful in a way that has nothing to do with light or angle or a good photo. It's the way you laugh with your whole face. It's the way you care for people quietly, without needing anyone to notice. God made you carefully, and I notice.

You've never once made me feel insecure. Not with a look, not with a word, not with silence. In a world that teaches people to keep each other guessing, you've only ever made me feel steady. I don't take that lightly. I think it's one of the quiet ways God has been kind to me — through you.

I'm not writing this to promise you anything. Jesus was clear about that in Matthew 5:37 — my "yes" should simply mean yes, my "no" should simply mean no; anything more than that comes from a place I don't want to build us on. So instead of vows, I'm giving you the truest thing I have: my sincere intention, today and for as many days as God gives me, to love you the way He asks me to — patiently, honestly, without keeping score.

What I want for us isn't just a good life together here. It's heaven. That's the actual goal. Every small thing — how we speak to each other, how we forgive, how we grow — is practice for eternity, not just for this life.

Yours, in Christ and in every ordinary Tuesday —
Roshan

The only goal that matters

Not a promise. A direction.

I won't stand here and swear things to you, because I don't want our love resting on my willpower — it should rest on something steadier. Scripture tells me not to make oaths at all, so instead of vowing, I'm choosing, daily, on purpose, to point us toward God.

That's the real target: not just years together, but heaven, together. Everything else — patience on hard days, honesty when it's uncomfortable, choosing you again tomorrow — is just the practice of walking that direction.

  • Seek His kingdom first, and let everything else — including us — be added (Matthew 6:33).
  • Let our "yes" mean yes, plainly and always (Matthew 5:37).
  • Hold on to the hope that one day, every tear is wiped away for good (Revelation 21:4).

A tiny rhythm you can actually keep

  1. Start the day with one minute of quiet before God — not a to-do list, just "here I am."
  2. Work in short, honest blocks. Diligence isn't marathon suffering; it's showing up again and again.
  3. When you feel the pull to scroll instead of study, say Philippians 4:13 out loud — "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me" — and start with just five minutes.
  4. End the day thanking God for what you did manage, not shaming yourself for what you didn't. Grace fuels discipline better than guilt ever will.
